About my Trip

The National Library of Ireland is a large research library located in Dublin, the capital city of Ireland. The library is home to a wide range of collections related to Irish history, literature, and culture. In addition to housing a large collection of books and manuscripts, the library also hosts exhibitions and events.

The photo in question was taken outside of the National Library of Ireland during a visit by the speaker and their Irish History class. The purpose of the visit was to view an exhibit dedicated to W. B. Yeats, a famous Irish poet and one of Ireland's most important literary figures. Yeats was the first Irish person to win a Nobel Prize in Literature, which he was awarded in 1923.

Yeats was born in Dublin in 1865 and grew up in a family with a strong interest in literature and the arts. He began writing poetry at a young age and went on to become one of the most celebrated poets of the 20th century. His work is known for its exploration of Irish folklore, mythology, and spirituality, and he is considered one of the leading figures of the Irish Literary Revival movement.

In addition to his Nobel Prize, Yeats was also awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by the United States government in 1964. He is remembered as a key figure in Irish literature and culture, and his work continues to be studied and celebrated by readers and scholars around the world.

This photo shows me standing in the city centre of Ireland, next to the River Liffy. The River Liffy is a major waterway that flows through Dublin and into the sea. Although it is a beautiful river, it is not safe for swimming due to the high levels of sewage that flows into it. In the past, the River Liffy played a crucial role in the trade and commerce of Dublin, as it provided a direct route to the sea and allowed Dublin to connect with other countries by water. Today, it remains an important part of the city's landscape and continues to be a source of pride for the people of Ireland.
